GIMM. What is it?

GIMM stands for Games, Interactive Media, and Mobile. It was founded by Anthony Ellertson in 2016, and its first class graduated in 2019. GIMM is one of the most unique degrees in the country. It's a degree that focuses on game design, but it's not just for game designers. It's for anyone who wants to work in the game industry, or any industry that uses interactive media. Graphic design, UX design, Web design, virtual reality, augmented reality, mobile app development, video editing, music. These are just a few of the wide variety of required courses within this degree. As seniors, your final project is a virtual reality game. You create your own music, your own 3D assets, your own code, and completely your own idea and turn it into a functioning game. You start this project in the second semester of your Sophmore year, and continue to build off of it using multiple courses until your senior capstone. Each class has multiple projects, the entire degree actually only has one exam within the Physical Computing course where you learn the basics of open-source software development. The rest of the grading system is based entirely off of personal assignments and group assignments. Each course typically has a minimum of one group project. The degree is structured to teach students how to work in the industry before you graduate. We are molded into game designers from the minute we walk in the doors. I have never learned anything so quickly than I have within this program. So far, after three semesters, I have basic knowledge of over five coding languages with no prior experience. I created functioning projects with each language and frequently a combination of them. This program has tuaght me so much within a small three semesters. I'm not your typical game designer, I'm an incredibly diversified individual with the ability to work in multiple fields thanks to my education. Frequently, I will question whether or not I will enjoy a course based upon my skill set and my intentions for my future. I have yet to be disappointed by a course, and I have yet to be disappointed by the knowledge I have gained from this program. Every course, I discover new interests. I never thought that I would ever enjoy coding or web design, but here I am designing this website and enjoying every second of it. I can't wait to see where I am in two years, I'm so thankful for my incredible Professors and the opportunities that this program has given me.
