About Me

Hello! My name is Mackenzie Burt, I typically go by Kenzie or Kove. I'm a diversified 2D concept artist and animator that's pursuing a unique degree called GIMM, focusing on game design and 3D modeling! I specialize in making characters and stories that people can relate to. My most apparent interest is that of the player community itself. I strive to provide and help build projects and interactive content that allows others to not only enjoy themselves, but perhaps even learn from the experience. I believe that a lot of people these days struggle with mental health, and I truly believe that interactive media and video games in general are an incredible way to help people connect with each other and escape the realities that negatively impact them. I'm a very bubbly and positive individual and I particularly enjoy making others laugh. I learn very quickly, but the best way for me to learn is getting thrown into the chaos. I thrive with or without structure, and tend to work harder when I'm overwhelmed. I've always worked two jobs while going to University full time, I can handle a lot of pressure and a lot of tasks. GIMM. What is it?

GIMM stands for Games, Interactive Media, and Mobile. It was founded by Anthony Ellertson in 2016, and its first class graduated in 2019. GIMM is one of the most unique degrees in the country. It's a degree that focuses on game design, but it's not just for game designers. It's for anyone who wants to work in the game industry, or any industry that uses interactive media. Graphic design, UX/UI design, Web design, virtual reality, augmented reality, mobile app development, video editing, music, 3D modeling and animation. These are just a few of the wide variety of required courses within this degree. As seniors, your final project is a virtual reality game. You create your own music, your own 3D assets, your own code, and completely your own idea and turn it into a functioning game. You start this project in the second semester of your Sophmore year, and continue to build off of it using multiple courses until your senior capstone. Each class has multiple projects, the entire degree actually only has one exam within the Physical Computing course where you learn the basics of open-source software development. The rest of the grading system is based entirely off of personal assignments and group assignments. Each course typically has a minimum of one group project. The degree is structured to teach students how to work in the industry before you graduate. We are molded into designers from the minute we walk in the doors. In two and a half years within this program, I've learned front end and back end web development (along with server side as well), 3D modeling and animation, game development, level design, audio design, graphic design, UX/UI desing and testing, and so much more. Most importantly, I've learned how to work with a team, how to lead a team, and how to figure out problems on my own and with others. The program is incredible, and I would highly recommend it to anyone.
